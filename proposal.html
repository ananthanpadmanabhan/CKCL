Data Requirements (Pilot)

Four lightweight spreadsheets. No GPS, fuel logs, or electricity meter data needed.

1) Materials Ledger (monthly; by facility and/or ward)

Columns:

  • month, facility_id, ulb_or_ward_id, material_category (PET — Polyethylene terephthalate; HDPE — High-density polyethylene; LDPE — Low-density polyethylene; PP — Polypropylene rigid; PP — Polypropylene film; MLP — Multi-layered plastic; paper — ONP/OCC; metals — steel/aluminium; glass; textiles; footwear; thermocol; e-waste group; rejects), inbound_tonnes
  • destination_type (recycling / co-processing / plastic_road / resale / residue), outbound_tonnes, batch_id, dispatch_date, partner_name
  • Optional: road_stretch_id, bitumen_substitution_rate, cement_partner_tsr (thermal substitution rate)
2) Quality and Process (monthly)

Columns:

  • month, facility_id, line_id, bale_purity_percent, contamination_percent, rejects_kg
  • If line-level unavailable, monthly totals by facility are fine.
3) Social Aggregates (quarterly)

Columns:

  • period, site_id, headcount_total, women_percent, ppe_issuance_percent (Personal Protective Equipment), training_hours_per_worker, on_time_payment_percent, incident_count
4) Traceability (monthly; simple counts)

Columns:

  • month, facility_id, total_outbound_lots, lots_with_complete_docs, data_completeness_score

Future Add-ons: Energy and Logistics

When ready, these modules slot in without changing the waste/social workflow.

Facility Energy Module

  • Energy intensity (kilowatt-hour per tonne) by facility and line
  • Electricity emission factor from Central Electricity Authority (CEA) Baseline Database
  • Key performance indicators: kilowatt-hour per tonne, tonnes CO₂e per tonne (Scope 2)

Data needed: monthly electricity bills or sub-meter totals per facility.

Logistics and Transport Module

  • Routing efficiency and load factor (tonne-kilometres, litres per 100 km)
  • Fuel emission factors and Scope 1 transport emissions
  • Key performance indicators: kilograms CO₂e per tonne-kilometre; diversion per kilometre

Data needed: monthly fuel receipts or telematics export (vehicle, distance, volume collected).

These add-ons are optional. We recommend piloting waste and social first, then enabling energy/logistics.

Methods & Sources (Lean and Auditable)

Destination-based avoided-emissions with conservative ranges. Credits are claimed only where documentation exists (we default to zero if in doubt). All figures reported as carbon dioxide equivalent (CO₂e).

Equations (plain text)

  • Recycling (by material i): Avoided CO₂e = tonnes recycled of i × (emission factor of virgin i − emission factor of recycled i).
  • Cement co-processing (fuel substitution): Avoided CO₂e = tonnes of waste used as fuel × thermal substitution rate × lower heating value × (coal emission factor − alternative fuel fossil emission factor).
  • Plastic-modified roads (bitumen displacement): Avoided CO₂e = tonnes of plastic used × measured bitumen substitution rate × bitumen emission factor.
  • Optional (with proof): Landfill methane avoided for paper/textiles diverted from disposal (IPCC defaults or EPA WARM v15 as applicable).

Factor Library (ranges; locked at pilot kickoff)

Published cradle-to-gate values; final factors are frozen for the pilot and cited in the Methods Note.

  • Plastics — virgin resin (kg CO₂e per kg resin): PET ~2.1–2.7; HDPE ~1.3–1.8; PP ~1.5–1.9. PlasticsEurope Eco-profiles · ACC HDPE LCA (2020)
  • Plastics — recycled resin (kg CO₂e per kg resin): rPET ~0.45–1.0; rHDPE ~0.4–1.0; rPP ~0.3–0.7. APR/Franklin Associates LCI (2018)
  • Paper (grade-specific, method-dependent): Use EPA WARM v15 for recycling vs landfill/combustion comparisons; values vary by OCC/ONP and disposal baseline. EPA WARM v15
  • Metals: Primary vs recycled factors from worldsteel (steel) and International Aluminium Institute (aluminium); ranges depend on electricity mix. worldsteel LCI · IAI LCI
  • Glass: Container glass LCA benchmarks (melting energy and cullet benefits). FEVE LCA
  • Bitumen for plastic-modified roads (t CO₂e per tonne bitumen): Use ~0.39–0.62 t CO₂e/t (conservative envelope; region-matched at kickoff). NAPA 2024 · Eurobitume LCA 4.0

India-specific guidance

  • Cement co-processing: Follow Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) protocol; credit based on measured thermal substitution rate (TSR) and lower heating value (LHV). CPCB guidance
  • Plastic roads: Use Indian Roads Congress (IRC) SP:98 and Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH) circulars; require documented bitumen savings for credits. IRC SP:98 · MoRTH circular
  • Grid electricity (if later needed): Central Electricity Authority (CEA) CO₂ Baseline Database for India. CEA baseline database

Quality & traceability standards

  • Bale quality (plastics): Bale purity and contamination measured against Association of Plastic Recyclers (APR) Model Bale Specifications. APR bale specs
  • Recovered paper grades: European List EN 643 and CEPI quality control guidelines for contamination and moisture thresholds. CEPI guidelines
  • Cross-commodity specs: ISRI Scrap Specifications Circular for metals, glass, paper and plastics. ISRI specs

Accounting framework

We follow the Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Protocol (Corporate Standard and Scope 3 Technical Guidance). All material-specific factors and methods are documented in the Methods Note delivered with the pilot.

GHG Protocol Corporate Standard · Scope 3 Calculation Guidance · IPCC 2006 Guidelines

Notes on uncertainty & boundaries
  • When multiple credible sources exist, we select conservative (higher) virgin and recycled factors to avoid overstating benefits.
  • No credit is taken for transport or energy unless metered data is provided; otherwise we report waste-only impacts.
  • Plastic-road benefits are claimed only when bitumen displacement is evidenced by mix design and purchase records.
